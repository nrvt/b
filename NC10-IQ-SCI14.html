How charcoal is different from wood?
Charcoal is a product of wood, whereas wood is the raw material.
Charcoal is formed by burning wood in absence of oxygen. Charcoal has higher energy density, produces less smoke & ash and is a more compact fuel when compared to wood.
Biogas is also known as gobar gas. Justify.

As cow-dung in Hindi is called as 'Gobar'; Hence Bio-gas is also known as gobar gas in Rural India.

2M Questions

Write two disadvantages of using fossil fuels.
The disadvantages of using fossil fuel are,
  1. Non-renewable energy sources
  2. Causes Environmental pollution
  3. Main reason for green house effect and global warming
  4. Released oxides of carbon, nitrogen and sulphur (acidic in nature) which causes acid rain that damages trees, plants, reduces fertility of soil.
What are solar panels? Write three uses of solar panels.
Solar panels are an array contisting of many solar cells connected in series and are used to generate electrical energy using solar energy.
The uses of solar panel are,
  1. In Artificial-Satellites and Space-Probes
  2. Radio, TV relay stations in remote locations
  3. In Traffic signals, calculators and in many other electrical devices
Name four gases mainly present in bio gas.
The four gases present in Bio-gas are,
  1. Methane
  2. Carbon-di-oxide
  3. Hydrogen
  4. Hydrogen Sulphide
Define nuclear fusion.
The process in which the heavy nucleus of a radioactive atom (such as uranium, plutonium or thorium) split up into smaller nuclei when bombarded with low energy neutrons, is called Nuclear Fission.
Write two limitations of using wind energy.
The limitations of using wind energy are,
  1. Not available continuously
  2. Cannot be used in all locations (only used in plains or on top of hills)
  3. Cost of setting up and maintainance is high
  4. Wind energy farms need large area of land

The advantages of geothermal energy are,

Why we pay attention towards alternative or non-conventional sources of energy?
The reasons why we pay attention towards non-conventional sources of energy are,
  1. The fossil fuel reserves in the earth are limited which may get exhausted soon if we use them at the current rate
  2. Reduce the pressure on fossil fuels making them last for a much longer time
  3. To reduce the pollution level and to save the environment
Write two advantages and two limitations of dams for the production of hydro electricity.
The Advantages of building dams for hydro electricity production are,
  1. Flowing water is a renewable source of electric energy.
  2. Construction of dams prevents flooding of rivers, provide water for irrigation.

The Disadvantages of building dams for hydro electricity production are,
  1. Large ecosystems are destroyed.
  2. Creates the problems of satisfactory rehabilitation of displaced people.

3M Questions

Charcoal is a better fuel than wood. Why?
Reasons why charcoal is better than wood are,
  1. It is more compact. Hence easy to store.
  2. It doesn't produce much smoke.
  3. It doesn't leave much ash. Hence there is less wastage.
  4. It has high calorific value(Energy density)
  5. It has lesser weight.
  6. It cannot rot/be eaten by insect like wood.
What is bio mass? How does bio gas plant help to reduce the problem of pollution?
The dead parts of plants and trees and the waste materials of animals and man are called Biomass.

The ways in which bio gas plant helps to reduce the problem of pollution are,
  1. As it can easily replace LPG for cooking purposes, it reduces our dependence on fossil fuels.
  2. Biogas burns completely hence there is no problem of disposal of smoke or ash.
  3. It is uses organic wastes as raw materials which otherwise would have caused littering, pollution of water, soil and wind.
  4. The slurry left behind in the plant is used as excellent manure rich in nitrogen and phosphorus. More trees means less pollution.
Write three advantages and three limitations of using solar cooker.
The advantages of using solar cooker are,
  1. Cheap to buy. Initial investment is negligible.
  2. Uses free & never-ending fuel in the form of solar light.
  3. Can be used in any part of the world.
  4. No maintainance as there are no moving parts.

The limitations of using solar cooker are,
  1. Cannot be used on cloudy days and at night
  2. Cooking is slow and takes a lot of time
  3. As sun changes its direction, the position of reflector needs to be adjusted regularly
  4. Cannot be used for baking or frying
Why it is not possible to make use of solar cells to meet all our needs? State three reasons.
It is not possible to make use of solar cells to meet all our needs because,
  1. Produces only DC current and to run AC appliances we need inverters which are costly.
  2. Availability of special grade silicon for making solar cells is limited and causes pollution.
  3. Batteries cannot store large amount of charge and also need replacement periodically.

5M Questions

Why tidal energy do not become the main source of energy?
The reasons why tidal energy have not become the main source of energy are,
  1. Tidal energy can only be used in specific locations where geography produces large tides.
  2. The difference in water level during Rise and fall of tides is not large enough.
  3. Saline water corrodes the gates of reservoir and blades of turbines.
  4. Meeting the daily demand cycle is difficult as tides follow 15 day cycle.
  5. Construction of large power plants and reservoirs near the seas and oceans is difficult.
  6. Tidal stations are always in danger from calamities like Tsunamis, Cyclonic storms & Earthquakes.
What are OTEC plants? Which two main points are necessary for its working?
OTEC plants are Oceanic Thermal Energy Conversion Plants which are devices used to harness difference in temperature in layers of ocean to generate electrical energy.
The requirements necessary for working of OTEC are,
  1. Temperature difference must be $20^oC$ or more.
  2. Minimum depth of water should be 2000m.
  3. Availibility of working gases like Ammonia and a warm surface to boil it in order to run the turbine.
Bio gas is a boom for farmer. Why?
Bio gas is considered as a boom for farmer because of the following reasons,
  1. Building bio-gas plant needs less investment.
  2. Bio-gas plant needs less space.
  3. It is a clean and safe fuel.
  4. Bio-gas can be transported and used for many applications like cooking, lighting etc.
  5. Uses organic wastes as raw material which help the farmer in waste management.
  6. The slurry left behind is organic manure rich in phosphorus and nitrogen.
  7. Manure can be sold to other farmers to increase soil fertility.
